🏛️ The Four Pillars

1. Opportunity Scanner

  • → Stalled deals: Open deals with no engagement in 7+ days, weighted by value and stage
  • → Cold leads: Contacts with prior interest (meetings booked, proposals viewed) who went silent 10+ days
  • → Missing next steps: Deals in active stages with no scheduled follow-up task or meeting
  • → Nudge candidates: Deals stuck in the same stage too long (stage duration vs. benchmark)

2. Recommendation Engine

For EACH flagged opportunity, generate:

  • → Why flagged: "No contact in 12 days, proposal sent but no reply"
  • → Recommended next move: "Send follow-up referencing [news article]"
  • → Urgency: 🔴 High / 🟡 Medium / 🟢 Low
  • → Confidence: How sure the recommendation is right (based on data completeness)
  • → Suggested owner: Current deal owner + backup contact

3. Outreach Draft Generator

Produce reviewable drafts for five outreach types:

  • → Reactivation email: Dormant account + timely hook (news, job change, industry trend)
  • → Follow-up email: Continue a conversation that stalled, reference last touchpoint
  • → Proposal nudge: Proposal sent, no response — gentle push with value reinforcement
  • → Meeting request: Suggest a specific time, reference the deal context
  • → Relevant check-in: NOT "just checking in" — anchored to a news article, LinkedIn post, or industry event that makes it timely and valuable

4. Mission Control Integration

Output appears as structured artifacts in the existing system:

  • → Task packets: Each flagged opportunity becomes a reviewable task packet
  • → CRM review queue: Ordered list of opportunities for the team to action
  • → Artifacts/drafts linked to tasks: Email drafts attached to the corresponding task

⚠️ Risks & Edge Cases

HIGH — Alert Fatigue

Too many flagged deals → team ignores everything

With 2,000+ stale deals, unfiltered output would be noise. Mitigation: Focus on active sales pipelines (Purchase Manual + Events, not Drafts/Onboarding). Cap at 10-15 high-urgency items per digest. Prioritize by deal value × staleness × stage.

HIGH — Draft Quality Variance

Bad drafts erode trust faster than good drafts build it

Mitigation: Deuce reviews all drafts for first 2 weeks. Voice matching only when 10+ sent emails exist. Always include ⚠️ disclaimer. Never include pricing or contract terms.

MEDIUM — Company Name Matching

News articles use different names than HubSpot

Mitigation: Alias table + fuzzy matching. Start with exact match, add aliases as mismatches surface. Log unmatched companies for manual review.

MEDIUM — Fivetran Sync Lag

CRM data could be 6-24h behind

Mitigation: Always display "CRM data as of [timestamp]" in output. If >12h stale, add warning. Run scan at 6 AM when sync is freshest.

MEDIUM — BigQuery Costs

Daily full-pipeline scan could get expensive

Mitigation: Pre-materialize daily summary table (one scheduled query). Interactive features read from cache. Set byte limits on all queries.

LOW — Pipeline Complexity

4 pipelines with different stages and meanings

Mitigation: Focus v1 on Purchase (Manual) pipeline (577 open deals, clearest stages). Add Events pipeline in v1.1 once logic is proven.

💡 Ideas to Make This the Most Powerful Agent on the Team

IDEA 1 — "BRIEF ME" COMMAND

Pre-Meeting Intelligence in 10 Seconds

Rep types brief me on PepsiCo in Slack → gets: recent news, deal status, last 3 interactions, open tasks, contact details, staleness flags, and recommended talking points. Replaces 15 minutes of CRM spelunking. This alone could justify the entire project.

IDEA 2 — DEAL VELOCITY ALERTS

Know When a Deal Is Accelerating or Dying

Track engagement velocity per deal (emails per week, meeting frequency, response times). When a deal suddenly goes from 3 emails/week to 0, flag it BEFORE it's stale. Conversely, when a dormant deal suddenly gets activity, flag it as "heating up — capitalize now."

IDEA 3 — RELEVANT CHECK-IN LIBRARY

Never Send "Just Checking In" Again

Every check-in email gets anchored to SOMETHING: a news article, a LinkedIn post from their company, an industry trend, a competitor move, a Recess case study that matches their profile. The agent maintains a library of "hooks" per company and rotates them so you never repeat the same angle.

IDEA 4 — WEEKLY PIPELINE PULSE

60-Second Audio Brief for Sales Leadership

Every Friday: "This week, 3 deals moved to Proposal Sent ($450K total). 5 went stale. Katie closed Haleon. Danny's Monster Energy deal is heating up — 4 inbound emails this week. Top risk: PepsiCo renewal hasn't been touched in 14 days." Use ElevenLabs TTS, drop in #sales-leadership. Executives will love this.

IDEA 5 — JOB CHANGE TRIGGERS

Your Champion Just Left. Now What?

Detect when a contact at a target account changes jobs (via LinkedIn monitoring or news). Two plays: (1) follow the person to their new company and bring Recess with them, (2) identify the replacement and introduce yourself. Job changes are the #1 reason enterprise deals die silently.

IDEA 6 — DEAL SCORE + RECOMMENDATION COMBO

Use Your Existing Deal Scores as a Signal

You already have analytics.combined_deal_scores with tiers A-F. Combine: "This deal just dropped from B-tier to C-tier AND hasn't been contacted in 9 days" = much stronger signal than either alone. Score changes become urgency multipliers.

IDEA 7 — "WIN BACK" CAMPAIGNS

Closed-Lost Deals That Deserve a Second Look

Scan closed-lost deals from 6-12 months ago. If the company is back in the news (new leadership, new budget, new product launch), generate a reactivation recommendation. "Molson Coors closed-lost 8 months ago ($250K), but they just announced Horizon 2030 with expanded marketing budget — worth a fresh pitch."

IDEA 8 — MULTI-THREAD ACCOUNT PLAYS

Don't Put All Eggs in One Contact

When a deal has only one contact and they go cold, suggest multi-threading: "Shannon at Haleon hasn't responded in 11 days. You have 2 other CLOSE WON contacts: Izabella Brooks (Flonase) and Ryan Collins. Draft a warm intro via one of them?" Prevents single-point-of-failure relationships.
